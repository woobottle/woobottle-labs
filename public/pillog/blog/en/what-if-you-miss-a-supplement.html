What should you do if you miss a supplement?

Short answer: If you miss a supplement, the usual approach is to take it when you remember if it's still the same day, or simply skip it and continue as normal — rather than doubling up to catch up. For medicines specifically, follow the label or ask your pharmacist, since rules can differ.

Almost everyone who takes a daily supplement misses a day sooner or later — a busy morning, a trip, a night out, or just forgetting the bottle at the office. The moment you notice, it's easy to feel like you have to "fix" it right away. In practice, a calmer approach usually serves you better than trying to catch up.

Don't double up to catch up

A single missed day of a supplement is rarely a big deal, and taking two at once to compensate is generally not the answer. In most cases, taking it when you remember (if it is still the same day) or skipping to the next scheduled time is enough.

The instinct to "make up for it" is understandable — it can feel like you're falling behind on something important. But supplements are usually meant to support a steady, ongoing pattern rather than to hit an exact daily target. Doubling a dose doesn't add the benefit of the missed day back in; it just means you're taking more than usual at once, which for some people can feel harder on the stomach or simply unnecessary. When in doubt about a specific product, the label is the most reliable source, since serving sizes and formulations vary.

Medicines can have different rules than supplements, so for anything prescribed, check the label or ask your pharmacist rather than guessing. Some medications do have specific "missed dose" instructions printed on the packaging or pharmacy handout, and those instructions should take priority over any general rule of thumb.

Reducing the misses

If you are missing often, the fix is usually the routine, not willpower. It's rarely about caring less — it's usually that the reminder cue (a specific meal, a specific bag, a specific time) quietly changed, and the habit slipped along with it. Seeing the pattern often reveals a simple change — moving the time to a meal you never skip, for example. Some people find mornings unreliable because breakfast itself is inconsistent, while an evening routine right before brushing teeth turns out to be much sturdier. Others do better keeping the bottle somewhere they'll physically see it, like next to the coffee maker or on the desk, rather than tucked away in a cabinet.

Starting small helps it stick

If missed days keep piling up, it can help to simplify rather than add more rules. Trying to remember three or four supplements at three different times a day is a lot to keep track of, and a single slip can cascade into skipping the rest of the day too. Pairing everything into one or two set moments — instead of scattering pills throughout the day — tends to be easier to sustain.

It also helps to treat an occasional miss as expected rather than as a failure. A month with a couple of gaps is still a month of mostly consistent habit, and consistency over weeks and months is generally what matters more than any single day. Frequently asked questions

Can I take two the next day to make up for it?

Generally no — doubling up isn't recommended for most supplements. Just resume your normal schedule.

Does missing one day undo my progress?

For most supplements the benefit comes from long-term consistency, so a single miss is minor. Focus on getting back on track.

What if I realize I missed it the next day?

If you notice only after a day has already passed, it's generally simplest to skip the missed dose and resume your normal schedule from the next scheduled time, rather than trying to fit in an extra one.
